Lush, dense forests cover the region, ranging from subtropical to temperate zones. These forests are home to diverse species of rhododendrons, orchids, and medicinal plants. Central Himalayas.

The Central Himalayas, with its towering peaks and diverse topography, host a remarkable variety of flora adapted to extreme altitudes. Alpine meadows, characterized by hardy grasses and wildflowers, carpet the landscapes at higher elevations. Iconic rhododendron forests add vibrant hues during the flowering season. The region is also home to unique medicinal herbs and aromatic plants. The lower elevations feature mixed forests with oak, pine, and deodar trees, contributing to the overall biodiversity of the Central Himalayan flora.

Western Himalayas.

The Western Himalayas exhibit a diverse and rugged terrain that influences its distinctive flora. From the lower foothills to the higher alpine zones, the region supports a range of vegetation. Deciduous forests dominated by oak, walnut, and maple trees cover the lower altitudes, transitioning to coniferous forests of pine and cedar at higher elevations. Alpine meadows adorned with a variety of wildflowers add to the scenic beauty. The Western Himalayan flora reflects the ecological richness of the area, emphasizing the adaptability of plant life to the challenging environments of this mountainous region.
